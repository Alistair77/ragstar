New Employee Onboarding Guide

Welcome to the team! This guide will walk you through your first 30 days.

Week 1: Orientation
- Day 1: Meet your team, receive your equipment, set up accounts (Slack, email, GitHub, Jira)
- Day 2: Complete HR paperwork, benefits enrollment, and mandatory security training
- Day 3: Product overview session with your onboarding buddy
- Day 4: Engineering setup — clone repositories, run the development environment locally
- Day 5: First small task with your onboarding buddy's guidance

Week 2: Ramp-Up
- Attend daily standup meetings and begin contributing to team discussions
- Complete the code review training module
- Shadow a senior engineer during a deployment cycle
- Read through the architecture documentation and runbook
- Submit your first pull request (even if it's just a documentation fix)

Week 3-4: Independence
- Own and complete a small feature or bug fix independently
- Participate in on-call rotation with a senior engineer shadowing you
- Present a 5-minute lightning talk on what you've learned to your team
- Schedule a 30-day check-in with your manager to review progress

Benefits Overview
- Health Insurance: Medical, dental, and vision coverage starts on day 1
- 401(k): Company matches 100% of contributions up to 4% of salary, vesting immediately
- Paid Time Off: 20 days PTO per year, plus 12 company holidays
- Parental Leave: 16 weeks fully paid for primary caregivers, 8 weeks for secondary
- Professional Development: $3,000 annual budget for conferences, courses, and certifications
- Wellness Benefit: $100 monthly stipend for gym memberships or wellness activities

Tools & Access
Your onboarding buddy will help you get access to all necessary systems. Key tools include:
- Slack for internal communication
- GitHub for code hosting and code review
- Jira for task tracking
- Confluence for documentation
- 1Password for password management
- Okta for single sign-on access
