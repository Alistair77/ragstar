Incident Response Runbook

Severity Levels

SEV-1 (Critical): Service outage affecting all users, data breach, or security compromise.
Response time: Immediate. Team must respond within 5 minutes of alert.
SLAs: Mitigation within 1 hour, full resolution within 4 hours.

SEV-2 (High): Partial outage affecting a subset of users, degraded performance, or feature unavailability.
Response time: Within 15 minutes.
SLAs: Mitigation within 4 hours, full resolution within 24 hours.

SEV-3 (Medium): Minor bugs, cosmetic issues, or non-critical feature degradation.
Response time: Within 1 business hour.
SLAs: Mitigation within 24 hours, full resolution within 1 week.

SEV-4 (Low): Questions, documentation requests, or feature inquiries.
Response time: Within 1 business day.
SLAs: Acknowledge within 2 business days.

Incident Response Steps

Step 1: Detection and Triage
When an alert fires or a user reports an issue, the on-call engineer acknowledges the page and assesses severity. Use the runbook to determine if escalation is needed. For SEV-1 and SEV-2 incidents, immediately create a dedicated Slack channel with the naming convention #incident-YYYY-MM-DD-short-description.

Step 2: Containment
Before investigating root cause, take immediate steps to limit impact. This may include rolling back a deployment, enabling a feature flag to disable a broken feature, scaling up infrastructure, or blocking malicious traffic.

Step 3: Investigation
Once contained, investigate the root cause. Check logs in Datadog, review recent deployments in GitHub, examine database query performance in RDS Performance Insights, and review error rates in Sentry.

Step 4: Remediation
Apply the fix. For code issues, submit a pull request with the fix and have it reviewed by at least one other engineer. For infrastructure issues, update Terraform configurations or apply manual fixes through the AWS console.

Step 5: Post-Mortem
Within 48 hours of an SEV-1 or SEV-2 incident, the incident commander must write a post-mortem document. The post-mortem should include: timeline of events, root cause analysis, impact assessment (number of users affected, revenue impact, downtime duration), action items to prevent recurrence, and a blameless narrative.

On-Call Rotation
Each engineering team maintains a weekly on-call rotation. Primary on-call handles all pages. Secondary on-call handles escalations and can step in if the primary is already engaged. On-call engineers receive a $200 stipend for the week they are on call. The on-call schedule is managed through PagerDuty.
